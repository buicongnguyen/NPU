iHW's ACiM Patent Stack: What It Solves and What It Does Not

Intelligent HW's granted hybrid-computing patent attacks a real analog-memory problem: asking one physical cell to hold many precise conductance levels. Its answer is to build one logical weight from simpler digits, accumulate each positional slice in analog, then reconstruct precision digitally. Later applications add adaptive converter range, grouped quantization, and signed-current cancellation. Together they form a credible accuracy architecture, but they are not public proof that end-to-end analog error is solved.

Evidence boundary

A real granted architecture claim is not the same as a verified product result

Best-supported conclusion: iHW has a technically meaningful way to avoid depending on a single highly precise multilevel memory cell. The granted architecture can make logical weight encoding more controllable, while the newer applications address converter range, quantization range, and signed-weight peripheral cost. Public records still do not establish full-model accuracy, yield, energy, latency, or lifetime behavior across production chips.

Confirmed legal record The core US patent is active

US 12,586,636 B2 was granted on March 24, 2026. Its independent claims cover a particular hybrid line-significance and reconstruction architecture, not every form of bit-sliced CIM.

Company-originated claim 8-bit ACiM silicon and MobileNet

An iHW recruiting page claims a world-first 8-bit-weight ACiM silicon verification and MobileNet demonstration. No public independent benchmark methodology accompanies that statement.

Still unproven publicly Production accuracy and efficiency

The patent record does not report complete datasets, chip populations, PVT corners, aging, yield, full-chip energy, or end-to-end latency.

Patent or official record Company disclosure Engineering interpretation

Read "8-bit" carefully

In the core patent, an 8-bit logical weight may be reconstructed from eight robust binary slices or from fewer multi-bit slices. It does not require one physical cell to reproduce 256 ideal conductance states. Logical precision, converter precision, analog effective precision, and end-task accuracy are four different quantities and should never be reported as if they were interchangeable.

Core patent mechanism

Trade one fragile analog level for several simpler positional digits

The architecture is hybrid by construction. Memory cells provide parallel analog multiply-accumulate work; converters and digital logic recover the significance of each slice.

1. QuantizeLogical weightChoose a signed fixed-point or integer representation.
2. DecomposeWeight digitsSplit the value into binary or mixed-radix digits.
3. ProgramCells and linesMap each digit to a cell state and a positional output line.
4. AccumulateAnalog MVMApply encoded inputs and sum current independently per slice.
5. ConvertADC per sliceDigitize each output-line current in a suitable range.
6. RebuildShift and addScale by positional significance, combine signs, and accumulate.
Conventional pressure point

One cell, many conductance levels

A dense direct mapping asks each device to separate many analog states despite program variation, read noise, nonlinear response, drift, temperature, aging, and process spread. More nominal bits do not automatically produce the same number of effective bits.

Patent response

Several cells or lines, fewer states each

Each slice can use a small, robust state set, including on/off. Positional significance carries the high precision, and deterministic digital arithmetic reconstructs the logical value after conversion.

Abstracted computationNotation distilled from the patent architecture
W[i,j] = sum_k d[i,j,k] * R[k]

I[k,j] ~= alpha * sum_i X[i] * d[i,j,k]

Y[j] = sum_k R[k] * ADC(I[k,j])

d[i,j,k]  digit stored in a physical memory state
R[k]      positional significance, such as 1, 2, 4, 8 ...
I[k,j]    analog output current for significance slice k

Worked example from the disclosed idea

If three digit-line currents convert to 9, 1, and 3, and their positional weights are 1, 2, and 4, digital reconstruction produces 9 x 1 + 1 x 2 + 3 x 4 = 23. The analog array need not create one current that directly encodes 23 with a single precise cell state.

High significanceRobust storageUse fewer levels or SLC-like states because an error is multiplied by a larger positional weight.
Positive pathSigned magnitudeMap positive contributions onto a matched physical or logical path.
Negative pathDifferential termSubtract a negative path digitally or cancel paired currents before the ADC.
Digital boundaryExact recombinationShifts, scales, sign handling, and accumulation are deterministic after conversion.
Residual riskMSB amplificationA high-significance slice error is also amplified, so robust storage and converter headroom matter most there.

The cost does not disappear. More slices can require more cells, output lines, ADC phases, routing, input cycles, digital shift-add work, and calibration metadata. Fewer slices save area and cycles but reintroduce multilevel-cell variability. The engineering problem becomes an explicit accuracy-area-energy-latency tradeoff.

Supporting inventions

The later applications target costs left around digit-sliced representation

Pending application

Adaptive output and ADC range

A multiplexer selects an output current, a grading step estimates its magnitude, and the circuit selects a matching I-V resistance/capacitance, ADC reference, or oscillator capacitance.

Benefit: reduce saturation on large sums and avoid wasting ADC codes on small sums.

Moved cost: grading, references, calibration, extra control, and possible multiplexing latency.

Pending application

Grouped weight quantization

A layer is split into several quantization groups, often aligned with output channels or output-line groups. Each group receives its own scale for quantization and dequantization.

Benefit: reduce error caused by one outlier-dominated scale covering a heterogeneous layer.

Moved cost: more scale metadata, grouping constraints, compiler decisions, and digital dequantization.

Pending applications

Signed-current cancellation

Positive and negative split-gate flash cells alternate on a shared bitline so the ADC measures a residual current rather than two independently converted sums. A related arrangement changes control-line direction and paired read modes.

Benefit claimed: reduce the ADC count to one-half or one-quarter of a prior separate-path design.

Moved cost: device matching, bias control, cancellation accuracy, small-residual resolution, and common-mode headroom.

Granted device patent

Chalcogenide drift and read window

A separate patent addresses composition, initialization, and read behavior for a chalcogenide memory cell, including threshold drift and stable operation.

Benefit: improve a device-level memory path that could support analog computation.

Boundary: do not infer that the disclosed flash ACiM product uses this separate material system.

Accuracy is a budget, not a bit label: task error combines model quantization, cell state, input encoding, array transfer, conversion, digital accumulation, calibration age, and unsupported-operation handling. A defensible result reports each contribution and the hardware or software paid to reduce it.

Interview and technical due diligence

Questions that turn patent language into a product contract

PrecisionWhat exactly is 8-bit?

Logical weights after reconstruction, cell states, ADC output, accumulator, software-equivalent model, or measured effective precision?

Physical costHow many resources per MAC?

Report cells, lines, ADCs, read phases, input pulses, shift-add operations, and spare capacity.

ImplementationWhich claims are in silicon?

Separate the granted core patent from pending ADC, quantization, and signed-weight applications and identify the implemented revision.

AccuracyWhere is the full ablation?

Compare FP32, digital INT8/INT4, software ACiM, measured raw ACiM, calibrated ACiM, and hybrid fallback.

PopulationWhat are the tails?

Ask for lot count, chip count, weak arrays, rejected devices, remapping rate, yield, and worst-case accuracy.

LifecycleWho owns calibration?

Define factory, boot, thermal, periodic, workload, aging, refresh, rollback, and field-update responsibilities.

SystemWhat remains digital?

Quantify analog coverage, unsupported operators, transfer boundaries, digital correction, SRAM, DRAM, and host work.

SoftwareWhat is the stable ABI?

Inspect importer, IR, compiler, simulator, artifact, runtime, firmware, driver, profiler, debugger, and version negotiation.

Final assessment

Technically plausible, complementary, and still evidence-limited

The core iHW grant solves a narrower and more defensible problem than "analog accuracy": it reduces reliance on fragile high-level-count cells by moving precision into positional representation and digital reconstruction. The pending ADC, grouped-quantization, and signed-current ideas address important adjacent costs. This is a coherent hardware/software strategy. It becomes a product-level accuracy solution only when the company shows complete models, analog coverage, correction cost, chip distributions, PVT and lifetime behavior, yield, and end-to-end energy and latency against clear digital baselines.

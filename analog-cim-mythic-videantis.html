Mythic and Videantis: What the Acquisition Solves

Mythic acquired Videantis GmbH on May 19, 2026. The deal adds a production-proven digital processor architecture and software stack to Mythic's analog compute-in-memory platform. That can make the system complete and create a digital path for sensitive operations, but it is not evidence that flash-cell error, converter error, drift, aging, or analog effective precision has been eliminated.

Research verdict

A strong system acquisition, not a demonstrated cure for analog error

The best-supported interpretation is build-versus-buy for the digital half of a hybrid computer. Mythic already used digital SRAM, SIMD, RISC-V control, routing, conversion, calibration, and compilation around analog matrix engines. Videantis adds a much broader VLIW/SIMD processor, explicit memory control, complete-network execution, a compiler/SDK, simulation and debug tools, and automotive production experience. This can contain accuracy-sensitive work in digital execution, but the companies have not published the partition policy, numerical contract, calibration interface, or measured post-acquisition results.

Confirmed disclosure Hybrid architecture is intentional

Mythic says analog will execute efficient matrix operations while Videantis digital cores handle control, attention, non-max suppression, codecs, SLAM, and other high-performance work.

Engineering inference Digital fallback can protect accuracy

A compiler could route sensitive layers or unsupported operators to deterministic digital cores. The public announcement does not confirm that this sensitivity-based policy exists.

Not yet demonstrated The combined accuracy claim remains open

No public report gives same-model digital-INT8 versus hybrid accuracy across multiple chips, temperature, voltage, calibration age, device age, and analog coverage.

Company disclosure Independent or academic source Engineering inference

Public paper boundary

As of July 11, 2026, this review found Mythic architecture presentations, product documentation, software documentation, demonstrations, press releases, and trade reporting, but no peer-reviewed Mythic silicon paper publishing end-to-end model accuracy across chip populations, PVT corners, calibration age, and device aging. Related flash-CIM papers explain plausible techniques, but they are not proof of Mythic's proprietary design.

Videantis can remove system gaps, but it can also expose the true analog cost

The acquisition helps most with flexible compute, scheduling, memory control, tools, and productization. Flash programming, converter quality, calibration, retention, and analog effective precision remain Mythic responsibilities. Boundary traffic and excessive fallback can erase the claimed analog advantage.

Mythic critical path

First prove converter and flash accuracy across devices and time. Then prove that the unified compiler keeps sensitive work digital without excessive boundary traffic. Finally report end-to-end accuracy, latency, energy, analog coverage, calibration, refresh, and fallback together. A peak analog TOPS/W number cannot answer those questions.

Public record

The architecture evolved from an analog accelerator toward a hybrid platform

2018 Hot Chips architecture

Mythic presented embedded-flash matrix memory, 8-bit weight levels, ADC output, digital input approximation, SRAM, SIMD, RISC-V, and a tile network.

2020 M1108 demonstration

Mythic described more than 27,000 converters, 8-bit conversion targets, internal variation handling, and ResNet-50 operation near a digital INT8 baseline.

2021 M1076 product brief

The 76-tile device advertised 25 TOPS, 80 million weights, 3-4 W typical model power, pre-qualified networks, and no external DRAM for model weights.

December 2025 Funding and rebuild

Mythic raised $125 million and stated that it had rebuilt its architecture, roadmap, software, and strategy. New performance figures were identified as internal benchmarks.

February 2026 Honda co-development

Honda announced investment and joint automotive-SoC development, combining Honda models and ECU expertise with Mythic's analog CIM and SDK work.

May 2026 Videantis acquisition

Mythic bought the German processor-IP company and retained all five founders. Financial terms were not disclosed in the public announcement.

Mythic accuracy stack

Public disclosures show several correction layers, but not a complete proof

Mythic has not publicly disclosed every circuit or calibration algorithm. The flow below separates stated mechanisms from the measurements that would still be required for an engineering sign-off.

1. ImportModel graphONNX, PyTorch, or framework graph enters an offline deployment flow.
2. NormalizeRanges and INT8Quantization and numerical-range choices bound converter inputs and outputs.
3. ProgramFlash weightsWeights are represented by programmed conductance levels; stored truth can support refresh.
4. StimulateDigital approximationEarly architecture material applies input bits separately instead of a monolithic input DAC.
5. ComputeAnalog MVMFlash currents multiply and sum in parallel inside the memory array.
6. Convert8-bit ADC targetCompact high-speed converters digitize outputs; calibration must contain mismatch and PVT error.
7. CompleteDigital tileSRAM, SIMD, RISC-V, routing, and software execute non-MVM work and move tensors.
Disclosed mechanism Problem it addresses What is publicly supported Remaining evidence gap
8-bit flash weight representation Maps a digital model into dense nonvolatile analog storage. Hot Chips material describes 256 levels per weight and conductance-based multiplication. No public distribution of programmed error, retention, endurance, or effective bits across production chips.
Refresh from off-chip true values Can restore weights after drift or retention loss. Mythic's 2018 explanation explicitly says flash resistance can be refreshed from stored truth. Refresh interval, detection policy, write cost, downtime, endurance, and field telemetry are not disclosed.
Compact high-speed 8-bit conversion Limits quantization error at the analog/digital boundary. Mythic reported designing and production-testing tens of thousands of on-chip ADC/DAC elements. No public INL, DNL, ENOB, offset, noise, temperature, voltage, yield, or aging tables.
Internal analog and digital calibration Compensates mismatch, manufacturing variation, and environmental change. Company statements and independent trade reporting describe calibration as central to reaching the 8-bit target. The calibration equations, per-chip state, runtime triggers, cost, and failure policy remain proprietary.
Range setting, quantization, and retraining Avoids clipping and makes the model robust to low-precision execution. Public descriptions show an offline graph-to-analog-INT8 compilation path and pre-qualified models. No complete public model table reports baseline, converted accuracy, analog accuracy, and calibration conditions.
Digital support inside each tile Executes control and operations for which analog MVM is unsuitable. M1076 documentation lists RISC-V, SIMD, SRAM, and NoC resources beside every analog engine. The supported-operator list, fallback precision, conversion traffic, and accuracy impact are not public.

"INT8 accuracy" is not "perfect 8-bit analog arithmetic"

A model can finish close to a digital INT8 baseline even when individual analog operations have fewer effective bits, because quantization, calibration, retraining, error averaging, and digital execution can absorb some error. A valid proof must report task accuracy against the same digital baseline and also expose effective precision, saturation, chip variation, PVT, retention time, calibration age, and correction cost.

What Mythic bought

Videantis contributes the programmable digital system around the analog engine

Digital processor IP

VLIW plus SIMD unified cores

Scalable identical cores execute deep learning, classical vision, signal processing, imaging, video, attention, non-max suppression, and SLAM.

Accuracy relevance: deterministic digital execution is a natural home for sensitive, nonlinear, control-heavy, and unsupported graph regions.

Production software stack

Compiler, SDK, simulator, and debug

The v-NN SDK performs data-type analysis, dataflow analysis, graph partitioning, multicore mapping, and optimization. The tool suite includes a C compiler, code generation, simulation, profiling, debugging, coverage, and runtime memory analysis.

Accuracy relevance: the tools can host a future sensitivity-aware hybrid partitioner, but the Mythic integration has not been published.

System and automotive maturity

Explicit memory and deterministic deployment

Videantis emphasizes local data, explicit memory management, predictable execution, automotive sign-off tooling, ASPICE history, and deployed processor IP.

Accuracy relevance: repeatable scheduling and observability improve validation, but field reliability of prior digital IP does not prove the new analog subsystem's numerical accuracy.

Important boundary: Videantis does not publicly claim a flash-programming, ADC-calibration, analog-noise, or drift-remediation technology. It solves digital completeness, programmability, data movement, tooling, and productization. Any direct benefit to analog model accuracy comes through partitioning, fallback, orchestration, and validation, unless Mythic later discloses a deeper integration.

Build-versus-buy analysis

The acquisition is strategically coherent because it buys time, software, and production discipline

High fitArchitecture complement

Analog MVM and programmable digital execution solve different workload regions and share a data-locality philosophy.

High leverageTime to product

Mythic obtains roughly two decades of processor-IP work, a mature toolchain, founders, and deployed automotive experience instead of rebuilding all of it.

IndirectAccuracy benefit

Digital fallback and partitioning can reduce exposure to analog error, but no public combined benchmark proves the gain.

Low direct effectCell and converter error

Videantis does not directly remove flash drift, programming spread, ADC/DAC nonlinearity, PVT sensitivity, noise, or aging.

High valueAutomotive readiness

Tooling, deterministic execution, existing production programs, and safety-process experience align with the Honda SoC schedule.

UnknownFinancial return

The public announcement does not disclose purchase price, consideration, revenue, integration cost, or financial targets.

Why acquire instead of license?

The transaction retains all five Videantis founders and keeps Videantis as a wholly owned subsidiary. That gives Mythic control of the architecture roadmap, compiler/runtime integration, safety process, customer support, and scarce engineering knowledge. A normal IP license would provide cores and tools, but less control over the cross-layer numerical contract that a new analog-digital machine requires. This is an inference from the disclosed structure of the deal; Mythic did not publish its internal build-versus-buy analysis.

Flash levels and refresh

Mythic, 2020-2021

Converters, calibration, and INT8 target

Boundary: no complete public benchmark table or converter characterization.

Trade reporting

Internal calibration is essential

Independent coverage describes analog and digital calibration inside M1076 and reports Mythic's close-to-INT8 accuracy claims.

Boundary: reporting based substantially on vendor briefings, not an independent silicon reproduction.

Industry critique

Effective precision and aging remain disputed

Semiconductor Engineering highlights concerns around multi-level flash precision, aging, manufacturing spread, analog noise, and ADC/DAC energy.

Boundary: the article does not identify the cause of Mythic's earlier commercial setback and should not be treated as a Mythic failure analysis.

Acquisition release

Videantis is the digital half

Mythic explicitly assigns dense matrix work to analog and flexible control, attention, NMS, codecs, SLAM, and other high-performance work to Videantis digital execution.

Boundary: combined silicon, scheduling, accuracy, and energy results were not published.

Videantis disclosure

Mature mapping and validation tools

The v-NN SDK performs data-type and dataflow analysis, partitioning, and mapping. The tool suite includes simulation, profiling, debugging, coverage, memory analysis, and automotive sign-off support.

Boundary: these are digital-platform capabilities; their integration with Mythic calibration is not public.

Engineering inference

Sensitivity-aware digital fallback is plausible

A unified compiler could route layers with narrow error budgets, converter saturation, unsupported math, or safety constraints to Videantis cores.

Boundary: plausible architecture, not a disclosed feature or measured result.

Current roadmap claim

100x energy efficiency remains a claim

Mythic's 2025-2026 releases cite internal benchmarks and a 100x advantage over GPU systems; Honda independently confirms co-development and investment.

Boundary: partnership validates interest and diligence, not the advertised benchmark without public methodology and results.
